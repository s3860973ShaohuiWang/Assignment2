CYBERSECURITY

We are always providing our private details to companies every day, whether it be our names, email addresses, home addresses or even credit card details. Yet how sure can we be that these details that we provide to companies that we trust may also be obtained by those who can use it against us? This is where Cybersecurity comes in.

What is Cybersecurity?

Cybersecurity involves techniques and processes designed to secure and protect various digital components such as computing devices, data, and networks from unauthorized digital access by hackers, Cybercriminals, etc. Cybersecurity can be implemented in multiple ways depending on the type of Cyberattacks the system is prone to.

What is a Cyberattack/Cybercrime?

A Cyberattack is when hackers attempt to obtain or damage data, networks, or systems by various means in order to generate profit. There are many different types of Cyberattacks such as malware attack, phishing, man-in-the-middle attack, denial of service attack, etc. A malware attack is when a user or victim unintentionally downloads any suspicious attachments with malicious viruses embedded in them causing corruption in the system. A phishing attack is when the victim is sent fraudulent emails or messages that appear to be coming from a legitimate source in order to obtain sensitive information or install malware. The man-in-the-middle attack is when the hacker gains access to the information path between the victim’s device and the website server to which sensitive information is being provided to. Denial of service attack (DOS) is attacking the web by bombarding it with millions of fake requests for information which will tie up the server, forcing it to reply to so many requests making it unable to perform its usual functions.

How does Cybersecurity work?

There are many factors that must be taken into consideration in order to prevent Cyberattacks. Using authentication mechanisms allows the devices to identify the authorised user. This is simply done by entering a username and an associated password which is used to identify the account and ensure that the user is an authorised individual. Authentication can be enhanced through two-way authentications where the user is required to log in using a username and password which auto generates a ‘one-time password’ through a server and is sent to a registered device. This way, there is an added layer of security where you are not only relying on the password but also a third device to which the hacker needs physical access to. Another way to ensure protection from Cyberattacks is by installing Antivirus software to look out for viruses, trojans, and malware. This software will scan the websites that are visited and connections that are made through the device to ensure that all activity is authorized and legitimate. Installing a firewall on a system or server ensures that only required services are running on the device. Disabling unwanted services limits the threats that the device is likely to face. These are only a few techniques designed to protect users from falling victims to Cybercrime.

Cybersecurity in the future

In the future, Cybercrime rates are going to be higher than ever as most homes will own smart devices like voice assistants, voice-activated lights, vacuum cleaners and so on which will be easy access for hackers who are trying to gain access to sensitive details. Although these gadgets do not contain any sensitive information themselves, they link to other devices that do, and these devices will become vulnerable access points for Cyberattacks. In order to prevent such incidents and protect users, these rising threats require skilled Cybersecurity professionals to ensure safety for the users. Strong incorporation of Artificial Intelligence (AI) can also be expected in Cybersecurity in the future as AI can improve the analysis and study of Cybercrime and enhance Cybersecurity techniques that are used to combat Cybercrimes and protect users.

Potential Impact of this development

Although AI systems can help to identify incoming threats, they can also be misused by Cybercriminals to discover vulnerable users and make them victims of their attack. Cybercriminals will gain the ability to control an organization’s electricity network or cut off the water supply after taking over its AI system. Identity fraudsters can use AI to generate deepfakes and pretend to be authorised users in order to gain access to the system. However, deepfakes can be overcome through constant verification systems such as tracking eye movement, advanced facial recognition, keystrokes, and so on to ensure that the user is an authorised personnel.

In order to prevent such incidents from happening, more job opportunities will open up for skilled Cybersecurity professionals. Cybersecurity is employed by organizations to protect their infrastructure from potential digital harms and threats to their networks or systems. According to Industry Statistics, the jobs that will be in high demand in the future are Network Security Engineer, Cyber Security Analysis, Security Architect, Cyber Security Manager, and Chief Informations Security Officer. These jobs all require skilled Cybersecurity professionals who have the ability to protect users from threats and Cyberattacks. Cybersecurity professionals use various techniques to protect users such as running vulnerability scanners to identify potential flaws within the infrastructure. They monitor the systems and observe the data flow to the internet and check for malicious activity within the network. After the weaknesses are identified, Cybersecurity professionals will test to validate the existence of those weaknesses, and repair and strengthen them by installing Firewalls or Antivirus software.

How will this affect you?

Although we cannot put an end to providing our personal data to organisations nor erase the data that we have already provided, it is important that we have control over who has access to it. Cybersecurity will continue to play a tremendous role going forward as we continue to engage with the world through technology. Cybersecurity will bring a certain level of assurance that the information that we provide is safe and secure and is being handled with confidentiality and care to some extent. This means that we will have more control over who can access our informations as well as our devices. We can avoid being targeted by hackers who are trying to gain access to our information for profit. Therefore, my family and I can ensure that we provide our payment details to legitimate companies when purchasing goods without worrying whether the information we provide is secure. My family will also be less likely to receive any fraudulent emails and if they do, they will be warned against clicking or downloading any malicious links that may cause damage. Furthermore, my family can rest assured that man-in-the-middle attacks will be infrequent and undisruptive to the traffic that is being received and sent to our households thus securing data that needs to remain anonymous. As a result of tightened improvements of cybersecurity, it would be assumed that further prevention of denial-of-service attacks would be implemented, allowing access to crucial services when needed. This can be considered essential regarding medical, police or fire services thus affecting everyone in need of these services.

Blockchain and Cryptocurrency

What does it do?

Information technology is changing the economic processes of the modern age. In this ever-evolving technological era, even our financial structures are digitizing. At the basis of every financial system are transactional records and organization of ledgers. Blockchain technology is paving the way for a new sub-field of economics, as well as revolutionizing the way businesses operate and general ledgers are recorded. Blockchain is a shared record that is irreversible and passed through a “chain” of users. This technology facilitates the transference of information immediately and transparently. Transactions such as payments, orders, deposits, and others are easily tracked.

Digitizing the record-keeping process provides an enormous benefit to financial institutions, as they spend a lot of money and time on organizing their various records. However, the benefit does not have to stop there. The burgeoning field of blockchain technology opens the door to a new form of currency, free from the constraints of a centralized financial authority. Cryptocurrency is essentially a digital form of money. However, it is not standardized globally and is not accepted by all financial institutions. This will undoubtedly change soon. Cryptocurrency is basically a digital money voucher, and so it can be easily regulated by blockchain without much need for human administration. Blockchain’s immutability provides a safe space for the exchange of this new type of currency.

Third parties that are usually held accountable (such as bankers and brokers) could be rendered obsolete in the distant future. This would be possible if the global currency standard transitioned into cryptocurrencies. While that sounds unrealistic, certain government bodies are fully embracing the idea and attempting to implement it. China, for instance, is currently in the process of implementing digital currency. Trials for “Digital Currency Electronic Payment” (DC/EP) are still running in many cities across China. However, the general consensus about this digital yuan is that it is not a typical digital currency. Cryptocurrency began as a rejection of central financial authority, while the DC/EP is controlled by China’s central bank and represents an increase in control and regulation. Regardless of its intention, China’s movement towards digital currency show that what was once considered a science-fiction dream could be a reality in as soon as three years.

The rapid speed and momentum of modern technological progress has made blockchain possible. The power of interconnectivity offered to us by the current state of the internet is the only reason we have access to this technology today. The speediness of blockchain transactions are made possible by a technology called “smart contracts”. It is a set of rules programmed into any given blockchain. Conditions for transactions are defined and automated. The major selling-point of blockchain is its efficient approach to keeping irreversible ledgers. This technology offers automatically executed record-keeping within set standards. There is no need to cross-check records, as that process is also automated, saving participants a lot of time and effort. Without “smart contracts”, blockchain would not have such powerful potential.

What is the likely impact?

Blockchain and cryptocurrencies will revolutionize economies on a micro and macro level. In the long run, all things that can be digitized will be digitized. Cryptocurrencies may very well wipe out all centrally issued currencies. While this may sound impossible, so did many technological concepts before their actual inception. E-mail is still in the process of making snail mail completely obsolete. People may no longer send or receive physical mail within the next few years. Even now, the only physical mail most people receive are either bills or advertisements. Seeing the impact e-mail had on a historically established system like the mailing system gives us hope for a similar effect from blockchain and cryptocurrencies.

On a business level, it will make record-keeping more efficient and direct. Not only will blockchain technology cut the costs of transactions and their subsequent ledgers, but it will also minimize the potential mistakes found regularly in the classic, error-prone methods of yesterday. When the middleman is cut out, the transactional process is speedier. This record-keeping does not apply to only businesses. All sorts of tracing and data collection is made possible by blockchain technology. IBM Food Trust (in conjunction with Raw Seafoods) currently uses blockchain to trace seafood in restaurants and grocers all the way back to the boats on which it was caught. This efficiently enables more accountability in the food supply chain. Other industries, such as healthcare, will benefit from blockchain’s immutable record-keeping capabilities, as well.

Error prevention and dispute resolution will be things of the past once blockchain overtakes our current, multifaceted ledger systems. Paperwork will be replaced with this more streamlined process. Eventually everyone will be affected by this new technology, especially considering how much time and money it saves. Any business, small or large, will be shooting themselves in the foot by sticking to their old record-keeping methods. Entire industries can be reworked around blockchain technology. Things such as photographs, payment records, and permits can be accessed by relevant parties with the click of a mouse. While this is doable now, very little organizational effort is put behind blockchain tech, making it the superior choice overall.

Autonomous Vehicles

What does it do?

Fully autonomous vehicles are still a whiles way from being a common eyesight on our roads and to understand the state of this technology, you have to understand the different levels of autonomy. The SAE (Society of Automotive Engineers) defines there to be 6 levels of driving automation. This ranges from Level 0, described as ‘No Driving Automation’, a level where the vehicle is completely controlled by a human although there may be different systems that help the driver, for example, the emergency braking system. This in contrast to Level 5 vehicles which do not require human attention and can do anything that an experienced human driver can do. As Level 5 is the end all be all, this is what and where autonomous technology are striving to become. As of the present day, you will find that most new cars are Level 2 autonomy which can be best described as Partial Driving Automation. At this level, the car can take control of the acceleration, braking and steering; this is best seen in features such as auto-parking. These features are found in vehicles from brands such as Audi, BMW, Mercedes, Toyota etc.

As of today, there is not a production car in the world that can be classified as Level 3 autonomous although Tesla’s new FSD beta is seemingly closing the gap. With real-time adaptability and ability to navigate through traffic, it is hard whether or not Tesla’s eligible EV’s already meet the threshold. Whether or not you agree that Tesla has already succeeded with creating a true autonomous vehicle, it seems to be that the public’s opinion is that they will be the first ones. This thinking is demonstrative in the price of their shares as we have seen it rocket in the last few years. Although being known for having poor build quality and locking features such as speed increases behind software paywalls, it has not stopped shares from being worth $65(USD) in Jan 2019th climbing to a staggering $880(USD) in Jan 2021. That is not to say that other influences such as marketing has not influenced the price, but it is hard to argue that their position on the automotive race has not boosted their valuation. With Tesla fronting the lead of autonomous vehicles, all negative connotations associated with autonomy is splashed back onto them. The ethics and morals behind machine driven cars has always been questioned; should the car save the driver or the pedestrian? There’s also other questions such as ‘Who’s to blame if a car driving itself kills someone? Is it the driver who wasn’t driving or the company that made it?’. There are not right and wrong answers when it comes to tackling such a hefty question like this but that does not mean we can leave the answer blank. This recently prominent as of the 20th of April 2021, a fatal incident in Texas occurred where a Tesla that investigators state were “100% sure” had no one driving it ran off the road, hitting a tree, bursting in flames, and resulted in the death of the two men inside. Although there was the “likelihood that someone was in the driver’s seat at the time of the crash”, its hard to say who is at fault.

To further the technology to prevent accidents like this, more extensive training of the autopilot neural network for better depth perception, object detection and semantic segmentation will need to take place. More small and micro-architectural improvements are needed on new silicon chips to maximise performance-per-watt in order to power the full-self-driving systems.

What is the likely impact?

This technology, if fully functional and capable of performing tasks associated with that of Level 5 autonomy would greatly impact and change society. If majority of cars on road were to be fully autonomous, the advent of a ‘vehicular cloud’ would circumvent traffic jams and serve as a tool for efficient traffic management. With intelligent computing systems on board and increased sensing power, vehicles would be able to relay information between one another creating a network that precisely pinpoints where each and every car will be. This allows for route planning whereas a collective the cars form of a cloud network sending each car down a path which it minimalizes traffic.

Road safety is likely to change as this new technology remarkably increases road safety as past issues such as blind spots are now irrelevant. Not only would it have great positive effects on traffic flow through suburban and metropolitan areas, but it would also dynamically shift the in-car experience.

Regardless of what cars look like now, there would need to be a reimagined look of human interaction between humans and machines. Without the need of items such as a steering wheel and pedals, it poses the need for other objects such as windows. For instance, it would be plausible that vehicles with no windows would act as a sort of transportable cinema where customers could relax, sit, and drink whilst the vehicles drive them to their required destination. The possibilities are almost endless as cars would no longer be ‘cars’ perse but be a room on wheels. This cultivates into a reduction of certain industries, notably the taxi industry which would inexistant, forcing a new wave of loss employment but also new employment. It would also be these very people that would be most affected.

How will this affect you?

In day-to-day life, autonomous vehicles are set to change everything. It is a systematic rewriting of the way people transport and how they will conduct their day. For myself, regardless of if I own an autonomous vehicle or not, having these self-driving vehicles be at my disposable on apps such as Uber will differ to how these same apps deliver presently. If traffic optimisation becomes as a result of autonomous vehicles as theorised due to cloud vehicle systems, it could ultimately cut travel time for places that I travel to.

The main difference for myself and many others will be to having to learn to trust machines to calculate complex decisions that can impact the safety of our lives. Not only this but not having control over the vehicle in any compacity (as depicted in Autonomous Level 5) as pedals, steering wheels, indicators would no longer be existent.

This type of technology will affect all family or friends depending on how far into the future we are looking. If we were to look 3 years into the future for example, this technology would have a fairly drastically low impact unless there were to be huge breakthroughs such as traversing another autonomous level. If we were looking much further such as 50 years for example, it’s quite obvious that it would change everything that has to do with transport.

The target market is individuals in between 16-26, as in this age people commonly drive cars or are learning to drive on their own. They use phones and smart technology far more than older generations leading to the likelihood of them having charged and working smartphones with them to gain their help.

The motivation to create this app is due to the increasing lack of basic car maintenance procedures and common emergency situations within younger generations. Description

The questionnaire will be used to determine the exact type of car that the app user drives, although there is a skip button available for the user to skip the questionnaire and get straight to the application. A very useful component of the questionnaire that is not available in as much depth as the common search is the videos, the exact car model and type will allow for the common issues regarding the car to have a video instruction to help guide the user through the exact movements and steps. Although the video implementation will be a later done component, the app will be made with images and written information and videos can be updated in as the application grows.

As getting information you need from the app is essential, there is also different language settings that will be available to cater to individuals who are more fluent in a different language. In relation to the video segments, we plan to provide subtitles under the video and as the user needs grow then different language presented videos can be added.

As it was mentioned before that the app will download the information in order to allow offline use for emergency cases, there is also options available to download videos and certain information about certain cars.
